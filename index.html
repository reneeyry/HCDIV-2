Example 1: Apple FY24 Income Statement Chart
Introduction: This chart visualizes Apple's FY24 income flow using a Sankey diagram to show revenue, costs, and net profit across product lines. Major lines include iPhone, MacBook, iPad, and Services. Each product’s revenue flows into total revenue, then through costs and profits, leading to net profit. Year-over-year changes are noted.

Data and Presentation Issues:
  • Data completeness: Comprehensive but visually overloaded due to many product lines.
  • Presentation: Dense revenue and cost distribution. Color use (red, green, gray) makes it appear crowded.
Improvement Suggestions:
  • Simplify information by separating product lines or using a bar chart.
  • Reduce colors to highlight key data like revenue-cost differences.
Example 2: Network Graph
This network graph represents David Letterman’s "circle of influence" on IMDb. The central node is David Letterman, surrounded by individual episodes of his show, highlighting his extensive TV presence. Outer nodes show other projects, emphasizing his entertainment industry network.

Data and Presentation Issues:
  • Data density: High, making individual nodes difficult to identify in the outer circle.
  • Chart suitability: Effective for relationships but becomes cluttered with many nodes.
  • Color issue: Dark tones make information less intuitive.
Improvement Suggestions:
  • Use color groups to distinguish different project types.
  • Simplify by showing core nodes only.
  • Increase label clarity for key information.
